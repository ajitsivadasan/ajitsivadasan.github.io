The Book

Uncoordinated

Why Work Exists
But Livelihoods Don't

The International Labour Organization estimates that roughly two billion people — more than sixty percent of the world's employed population — earn their livelihoods outside formal employment structures. They are not unemployed. They are working, often continuously, but without the systems that make effort legible, income predictable, or trust portable.

This is not a failure of ambition. Not a lack of skill or willingness to work hard. It is a structural failure — rooted in how modern systems discover work but fail to organize it into sustained, dignified livelihoods. As employment absorbs fewer people and work fragments across platforms and informal markets, coordination collapses. Time is wasted. Trust resets. Volatility becomes normal.

Uncoordinated is the argument that coordination is infrastructure — and that redesigning it is not idealism, but engineering.
