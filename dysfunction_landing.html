The Book

Marketing didn't fail.
Its infrastructure did.

Over a decade, brands handed their most valuable assets — consumer attention, purchase data, distribution, and creative leverage — to platforms that optimized for engagement over outcomes. The transfer was quiet, incremental, and almost invisible until it wasn't.

DYSFUNCTION traces how Facebook, Amazon, Google, and the creator economy rewired the rules of brand building while most marketing organizations kept running the same playbook. It shows why AI isn't the cause of the crisis — it's the accelerant that makes every structural weakness impossible to ignore.

And it prescribes exactly what a marketing organization built for the agent era needs to look like.

The Author
