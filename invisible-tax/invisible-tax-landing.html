The Argument
"The measurement was accurate. The architecture was wrong."

Most marketing organizations are not measuring the wrong things by accident. The tools are precise. The dashboards are clean. The quarterly reviews are confident. And the underlying architecture is structurally misaligned with how value actually accrues.

That misalignment is a tax. It doesn't show up immediately. It compounds. Every optimization pass makes it worse. Every confident presentation reinforces it. Every strategy cycle buries it a little deeper.

By the time it's visible on the P&L — as brand erosion, as a CMO who hit every target and still got fired, as a board that funded growth that wasn't there — the decisions that caused it are three strategy cycles behind you.

The Invisible Tax is a forensic examination of why this happens, how to see it in your own organization, and what it actually costs when no one does.
